Government Agency Website Redesign

About This Case Study

This project was a redesign challenge, we were instructed to choose a government agency website that we took interest in. The project was to redesign and update their website as if they hired us. We were given five weeks to execute the design process: research, define, ideate, prototype, and test a responsive website solution.

Phase 1: Research

Problem

The current website is far from user friendly, difficult to navagate and
hard to find information the audience would be looking for

Audience

Environmental Advocates
Home Owners
Current Events Interest

User Research

My user research participants were asked to first get a feel for the site, they were then asked to conduct a variety of tasks. We wanted to see how users navigated through out the website and what issues they were coming across.

I gathered the following insights:

  1. All had throuble with finding what they need in the navagation bar
  2. Were getting lost in the website pages on multiple occasions
  3. Overwhelmed with the amount of information on the pages
  4. If they had to repeat the task they just completed, they would have no idea how to do it.

Phase 2: Ideation

Ideation

The process of ideation started by organizing the website by card sorting to simplify the flow and create a sitemap. We then started sketching out wireframes to try different layouts and to also see how to consolidate all the important information to users.

Once the wireframes came together in XD we continued testing and iterating until we were ready to bring the site to life.

Phase 3: Designing

Hi-Fi Wireframes

After the lo-fi wireframes we created a style tile to help us design the hi-fi pages also to make sure the colors complimented one another.

Once the pages started to all come together I continued to do testing and iterating to make sure the user experience was smooth and easy.
