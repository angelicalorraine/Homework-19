Department of Energy

Government Agency Website Redesign

About This Case Study

This project was a redesign challenge, we were instructed to choose a government agency website that we took interest in. The project was to redesign and update their website as if they hired us. We were given five weeks to execute the design process: research, define, ideate, prototype, and test a responsive website solution.

Research

During the research portion we annotated different pages for usability issues, heuristic issues, and also identified UI design patters, issues. We would also annotate parts of the pages that we had design recommendations that would make the user experience more intriguing.

Lo-Fi Wireframes

I started the redesign process by designing lo-fi wire frames to see how we can make usability easier and also appealing to the eye.

Hi-Fi Wireframes

Once the Lo-fi wireframes were complete, it was quiet easy inputting data and information into the layout, the color scheme was also chosen by keeping the "energy" colors just having them compliment eachother was the only issue I came into.

The Process

Along side of the redesign process, we had to figure out the best flow for users because the previous flow was extrememly confusing. The cardsorting portion of the project was definitely the most difficult part.

Testing

There were many mistakes found within the design of the navigation, all of the hover designs were different from the original look. So all mistakes were corrected and all fonts, looks, and size are the same on every page.

Key Takeaways

There are many takeaways within the lessons of web design especially incorporating RWD and remembering to design mobile first to help your web design be easier to format.
